How to Get Your DoorDash Income Documentation

Method 1: Official DoorDash Tax Documents

Best for: Strongest verification, always accepted

  1. Access your 1099-NEC: Open DoorDash app → Account → Tax Information → View/Download 1099
  2. Timing: 1099 forms are available by January 31st for previous year earnings
  3. What it shows: Total annual earnings from DoorDash
  4. Combine with: Your filed tax return (1040 + Schedule C)

Limitation: Only shows last year's income, not current earnings. If you started dashing recently or earn more now, supplement with bank statements. For more information on gig worker taxes, visit the IRS Gig Economy Tax Center.

Method 2: Bank Statements

Best for: Showing current, consistent income

  1. Download 3-6 months of statements from your bank's website
  2. Highlight DoorDash deposits (they'll show as "Stripe" or "DoorDash")
  3. Calculate monthly totals and note them on each statement
  4. Show pattern: Landlords want to see consistent weekly deposits

Important: Don't submit bank statements with personal spending visible without context. Some dashers create a dedicated business account for gig income—makes documentation cleaner and shows professionalism.

Method 3: Professional Income PDF (Fastest)

Common Mistakes LA DoorDash Drivers Make on Rental Applications

Mistake #1: Only Showing One Month of Income

Why it fails: Landlords need to see consistent earnings, not a single good week.

Fix: Always show 3-6 months of documentation. Include both strong and average months to demonstrate realistic income.

Mistake #2: Submitting Raw Screenshots

Why it fails: Phone screenshots look unprofessional and are easy to edit/fake.

Fix: Create professional PDF documentation. Use GigProof or manually format your earnings into a clean summary.

Mistake #3: Not Including Tax Documents

Why it fails: Without 1099/tax returns, landlords can't verify your income is legitimate.

Fix: Always include your most recent 1099-NEC and filed tax return, even if it shows lower income than current earnings.

Mistake #4: Hiding Multi-App Income

Why it fails: You're leaving money on the table—literally.

Fix: If you run DoorDash + Uber Eats + Instacart, document ALL platforms. Combined income of $2,800 + $1,800 + $900 = $5,500 looks much stronger than just showing $2,800.

Mistake #5: Applying to Wrong Price Point

Why it fails: If you earn $4,500/month, applying for a $2,200/month apartment ($6,600 required at 3x) will likely get rejected.

Fix: Do the math first. At $4,500/month income, target $1,500-$1,800/month apartments (2.5-3x comfortable).

LA-Specific Application Tips

Tip 1: Apply During Award Season (January - March)

Your earnings will be highest during awards shows, industry events, and Super Bowl weekend. Apply when you have your strongest 3-month track record to show.

Tip 2: Target Smaller Landlords

Large corporate property managers have rigid income requirements. Individual landlords in East LA, Koreatown, and North Hollywood are often more flexible with gig worker income.

Tip 3: Offer Extra Security Deposit

If your income is slightly below requirements, offering first + last month + extra security deposit shows financial stability and can close the gap.

Tip 4: Include a Cover Letter

Explain your work professionally: "I work full-time as an independent delivery contractor through DoorDash, serving the greater Los Angeles area. California has over 1.2 million gig workers, and food delivery is a stable, growing industry in LA."

Tip 5: Know Your Rights

California has strong tenant protections. Landlords cannot discriminate against you for being self-employed. If you meet income requirements with proper documentation, you should be considered equally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much do Los Angeles DoorDash drivers earn per month?

A: Full-time LA dashers earn $4,000-$6,000/month working 40-50 hours. Part-time dashers (20 hours) earn $1,800-$2,800. Peak earnings in West Hollywood and Beverly Hills during dinner hours can push hourly rates to $32-40/hour active time.

Q: Do LA landlords accept DoorDash income as proof?

A: Yes, when properly documented. You need tax returns (1040 + Schedule C), 3-6 months of bank statements showing deposits, and professional income PDFs. Screenshots alone have only ~25% acceptance rate in LA's competitive market.

Q: What income do I need for an LA apartment as a DoorDash driver?

A: Most landlords require 2.5-3x monthly rent. For a $2,000/month apartment, you need $5,000-$6,000/month in documented income. Full-time dashers can qualify for apartments up to about $1,600-$2,000/month.

Q: Which LA neighborhoods are best for DoorDash drivers?

A: Koreatown ($1,900/month rent) has the best value—affordable rent with extremely high 24/7 restaurant density. East LA offers the lowest rents. North Hollywood provides Valley accessibility. West Hollywood has highest earnings but expensive rent.

Q: Can I combine DoorDash with other app income?

A: Absolutely—and you should. Document income from DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart, Grubhub, and any other platforms. Combined income shows your total earning power and helps meet LA's high income requirements.

Q: How do I get my 1099 from DoorDash?

A: DoorDash provides 1099-NEC forms by January 31st for the previous year. Access it in the DoorDash app under Account → Tax Information, or through Stripe Express. You must have earned $600+ to receive a 1099.

Q: What if I just started dashing and don't have 6 months of income?

A: Show what you have (even 2-3 months), plus income from previous employment if applicable. Offer a larger security deposit, get a co-signer, or target apartments with more flexible requirements.
