How to Get Your Instacart Income Documentation

Step 1: Access Instacart Earnings History

  1. Open Instacart Shopper app
  2. Tap Earnings at the bottom
  3. View your weekly earnings (batch pay + tips)
  4. Screenshot each week for the past 3-6 months
  5. Note: Each week shows total earnings, batches completed, and active hours

Step 2: Download Your 1099-NEC

  1. Instacart provides 1099-NEC if you earned $600+ in the year
  2. Available by January 31st for previous tax year
  3. Access through Shopper app: Account ‚Üí Tax Information
  4. Or through Instacart's tax partner website (Stripe Express)
  5. This shows your total annual earnings including all tips

Step 3: Gather Bank Statements

  1. Download 3-6 months of statements from your bank
  2. Instacart deposits appear as "Instacart" or "Stripe"
  3. Weekly deposits should match your app earnings
  4. Highlight these deposits for easy landlord review
  5. Calculate and note monthly totals on each statement

Step 4: Add Other Platform Income

If you also work DoorDash, Shipt, or other apps:

  1. Gather earnings documentation from each platform
  2. Bank statements will show separate deposits from each
  3. Create combined income total showing all platforms

Common Mistakes Miami Instacart Shoppers Make

Mistake #1: Only Showing Base Pay (Not Tips)

Why it fails: Tips are 40-60% of your income. Hiding them makes your earnings look much lower.

Fix: Show TOTAL earnings including tips. Your 1099-NEC and bank deposits include tips‚Äîuse these numbers.

Mistake #2: Not Combining Multi-App Income

Why it fails: Showing $1,800/month Instacart alone looks weak when you actually earn $4,200 total.

Fix: Document ALL platforms. $1,800 IC + $1,500 DD + $900 Shipt = $4,200. That's the number landlords should see.

Mistake #3: Using Only App Screenshots

Why it fails: Screenshots are easily edited and look unprofessional.

Fix: Combine screenshots with bank statements (proves deposits) and tax documents (proves reporting to IRS).

Mistake #4: Ignoring Seasonal Variations

Why it fails: Showing only your best month looks suspicious.

Fix: Show 6 months including slower periods. Include cover letter explaining: "Holiday season earnings were higher. My conservative annual average is $X/month."
