Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much do LA Instacart shoppers earn monthly?

A: Full-time LA shoppers earn $3,200-$4,800/month. Erewhon-focused shoppers can hit $4,000-$5,500. Multi-app shoppers (Instacart + DoorDash) earn $4,500-$6,500/month.

Q: Do LA landlords accept Instacart income?

A: Yes, with proper documentation: tax returns, 1099s, bank statements, and professional income PDFs. LA landlords are familiar with gig income given the large shopper population.

Q: Which stores pay best for Instacart in LA?

A: Erewhon pays highest ($45-80+ per batch with tips), followed by Whole Foods, Bristol Farms, and Gelson's. Costco offers good batch pay but requires more physical work.

Q: Where should I live as an LA Instacart shopper?

A: Near high-tip stores: Culver City or Silver Lake for mid-range rent near Whole Foods, or West Hollywood if you can afford higher rent near Erewhon. Koreatown offers budget-friendly central access.

Q: What income do I need for an LA apartment?

A: LA requires 2.5-3x monthly rent. For a $2,000/month apartment, you need $5,000-$6,000/month. Combining Instacart with other apps helps meet this requirement.

Q: How do I get my Instacart income documents?

A: Access earnings in the Shopper app under Earnings → View History. Your 1099-NEC is available by January 31st if you earned $600+ the previous year.

Q: Can I combine multiple app incomes?

A: Yes—and you should. Document Instacart, DoorDash, Shipt separately, then present total combined income. This strengthens your application significantly.
