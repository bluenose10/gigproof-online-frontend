Where to Find Your Uber Income Documents

1. Uber Driver Dashboard (drivers.uber.com)

Your primary source for all income documentation:

  1. Go to drivers.uber.com and log in
  2. Navigate to Tax Information
  3. Download:
    • Annual Tax Summary - Total earnings, miles driven, trips completed
    • 1099-NEC - Official IRS form (available if you earned $600+)
    • 1099-K - May receive if you had 200+ rides AND $20K+ earnings

2. Weekly Earnings Statements

  1. In the Uber Driver app, go to Earnings
  2. View Weekly Summaries for each week
  3. Screenshot or export the last 3-6 months
  4. Note: These show gross earnings before Uber fees

3. Bank Statements

  1. Download 3-6 months from your bank's website
  2. Uber deposits will show as "UBER" or "RASIER"
  3. Highlight these deposits for easy identification
  4. Calculate monthly totals

Pro Tip: Bank statements are crucial because they show actual money received. Uber earnings statements show gross fares, but bank deposits show what you actually got paid after Uber's fees. Landlords and lenders care about your actual income. For more guidance on self-employment income, see the IRS Gig Economy Tax Center.

Common Questions About Uber Income Verification

Q: Does Uber provide employment verification letters?

A: No. You're an independent contractor, not an employee. Uber does not provide employment verification letters, income verification letters, or proof of employment documents. You must use tax documents, bank statements, and professional income summaries instead.

Q: When does Uber send 1099 forms?

A: By January 31st for the previous tax year. You'll receive 1099-NEC if you earned $600+ in the year. Access it at drivers.uber.com → Tax Information.

Q: What if I just started driving and don't have tax returns?

A: Use bank statements showing Uber deposits plus your professional income PDF. Offer larger security deposit if applying for apartments. Build 3-6 months history before major applications if possible.

Q: Should I show gross earnings or net income?

A: Show gross earnings (total before expenses) for initial qualification, but be prepared to explain net income (after expenses) for tax purposes. Bank statements show net deposits (what you actually received).

Q: What if I drive Uber AND Lyft?

A: Document both separately with their respective 1099s and earnings statements. Then present combined total income. Two sources often looks more stable than one. The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau has helpful resources on documenting income for loans.

Q: Can I use Uber income for a mortgage?

A: Yes, but typically you need 2 years of self-employment history shown on tax returns. Mortgage lenders average your Schedule C net income over 2 years.

Q: What's the difference between 1099-NEC and 1099-K?

A: 1099-NEC reports non-employee compensation (most common for drivers). 1099-K reports payment card transactions (received if you had 200+ rides AND $20K+ in payments). You may receive both or just 1099-NEC.

Tips for Stronger Income Verification

Tip 1: Use a Dedicated Bank Account

Having a separate account for gig income makes bank statements cleaner. All deposits are clearly from Uber/Lyft, no personal transactions mixed in.

Tip 2: Document Consistently

Keep monthly records of your earnings. When application time comes, you'll have organized data ready to compile.

Tip 3: Include Trip/Hour Data

Uber's Tax Summary includes total trips and online hours. Including this data shows the consistency and scale of your work.

Tip 4: Explain Seasonal Variation

If certain months are lower (post-holiday, etc.), include a brief note explaining this is normal seasonal variation in the rideshare industry.

Tip 5: Combine Multiple Platforms

If you drive Uber, Lyft, do DoorDash, etc., present all income together. Multiple sources = more stable total income.
