What it is: Your annual earnings statement from Instacart for tax purposes

How to get it:

What If You Just Started Shopping for Instacart?

If you've been shopping for less than 6 months, you can still get approved:

Strategy for New Instacart Shoppers:

  1. Show what you have: Even 1-3 months of earnings is better than nothing
  2. Provide previous employment proof: W-2s or pay stubs from your last job
  3. Show strong savings: Bank balance proving you can cover several months of rent
  4. Target flexible landlords: Individual landlords are more understanding than large property companies
  5. Offer to pay more upfront: First month + last month + deposit
  6. Consider a co-signer: Someone with traditional employment

Frequently Asked Questions for Instacart Shoppers

Q: Where do I find my Instacart earnings history?

A: Log into the Instacart Shopper app or web portal, go to Earnings, and you can view weekly and monthly totals. To create a professional document from this, use GigProof to extract and format it automatically.

Q: Does Instacart provide income verification letters?

A: No, Instacart doesn't provide employment verification letters like traditional employers. You'll need to use your 1099 form, bank statements, and create your own professional income summary.

Q: Can I use my Instacart earnings before I file taxes?

A: Yes, show your current earnings via bank statements and a professional income summary. You'll have your 1099 after January, but you can apply for apartments year-round using other documentation.

Q: What if my Instacart income varies week to week?

A: Show your average over 3-6 months and explain seasonal variations if applicable. Also show bank account balance proving you have reserves for slower weeks.

Q: Do I need a business license to rent an apartment as an Instacart shopper?

A: No, you don't need a business license to rent. Instacart shoppers are independent contractors, not business owners. Your 1099 and income documentation are sufficient.
